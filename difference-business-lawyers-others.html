What Sets Small Business Attorneys Apart from Others

For a start, a business lawyer is one who specializes in issues and disputes that affect businesses. These include taxation, business transactions, intellectual property, and business registration, to mention but a few.

They do not differ widely from other legal practitioners. However, certain traits and occupational requirements set them apart from the others. It is these inherent differences that form the subject of the discussions that follow.

The rest of the article shall indeed be devoted to an in-depth examination of the unique traits, duties, and responsibilities of business attorneys more so when compared to other kinds of attorneys.

What sets business attorneys apart from others?

Business attorneys differ from other cadres of lawyers in the following regards:

Training                                                  

Business lawyers undergo the same training as their peers in other legal fields. They, however, have a bias towards business fields. The entire curriculum, right from the pre-law stage through to the undergraduate, and finally to the postgraduate level is all leaned towards business. This is deliberately done to equip them with the right know-how they require to fulfill their careers well. Their bar exams are also tailored to factor this business bias.

Professional Skill Requirements

Apart from legal training, business attorneys are expected to possess certain skills that are suitable for their practice. These include analytical reasoning, critical thinking, excellent research, persuasive/negotiation skills, and above-board writing skills. This is due to the sheer nature of the field of business field.

Scope of the Legal Contract

As has already been alluded to previously, the business attorneys are exclusively concerned with business issues. As such, they are significantly different in their specialization as compared to other cadres of lawyers. They mainly aid businesses in registering, filing their tax returns, complying with legal/statutory requirements, battling court cases, and upholding fair business practices. These differ sharply from other lawyers such as child lawyers, criminal lawyers, civil lawyers, and indeed, more besides.

Experience

Being an intricate undertaking, business attorneys must also possess some amount of work experience in order to discharge their duties effectively. Most law firms will usually prefer those with a postgraduate degree such as a Master of Law. Almost all will normally want those who have practiced law at a lower level for some time. This is to ensure a fair mastery of all the intricate legal and business jargon that is incident to business law. It is also to guarantee clients of nothing but the very best possible services. This requirement may not hold for some other kinds of attorneys.

Clientele

It obviously goes without saying that their clientele is also different from the other lawyers. They mainly interact and serve businesses, kiosks, tax collectors, local authorities, small-scale vendors, retailers, wholesalers, and small-scale suppliers. They never serve children, large corporations, criminal suspects, and other kinds of litigants that have nothing to do with business.

Saturation

All in all, the profession of a business attorney is a highly-saturated one. This stems from the high number of potential clients at any given time. As such, more law students will naturally have a bias towards this field as opposed to others. Moreover, its curriculum is comparatively simple and is as such, quite easier to pass. This also contributes to the relatively higher number of such kinds of lawyers.

Remuneration

As of the year 2016, the average annual salary for all lawyers was $115,820. However, that of the business attorneys was $108,000. This means that business attorneys earn slightly less than their peers. This is understandable given that most of their clients are persons who lack that much financial power. They also do engage in plenty of pro bono services as compared to most of their peers. This definitely has an impact on their total possible earnings.

Geographical Spread

As expected, most business attorneys are based in urban areas. This is due to the fact that most of their potential clients are located there. This may not necessarily hold for other cadres of lawyers. This again is due to the fact that a good number of their clients are located both in the rural and urban areas in equal measure. Apart from the rural-urban spread, most business attorneys are also concentrated in populated areas. This is due to the expected higher number of businesses located therein.

Regulation and Licensure

Different urban areas where business attorneys are mainly located are governed by a distinct set of legal regimes. Because of these, the rules that govern the licensure and regulation of business attorneys may vary widely from one locale to another one, even within a state! Other kinds of lawyers are not so much affected by this divergent legal jurisprudence or jurisdictions. Some kinds of lawyers such as constitutional lawyers are in fact subservient only to one regulation i.e. federal government/the US Constitution.

Closing Remarks

In closing, business attorneys play a very vital role and contribution to any economy. Their uniqueness is, therefore, an issue worth knowing of. Of equal importance is the fact that not all of their uniqueness has been discussed. This is due to the limited space and time. Indeed several others had to be inevitably left out.
